Born a Crime - Chapter 1 and 2 Summary

In the first chapter Trevor Noah was taking about his birth in a time of apartheid in South Africa.
Being born to a black mother and a white Swiss father during such a racist regime made his very existence illegal.
He goes on to recount his early childhood, and the constant struggles his family faced due to their mixed-race status.
They lived in fear of being caught by the authorities, navigating their way through life
with the ever-present threat of being punished for something as simple as existing.

In chapter two, Trevor talked about his early years growing up in Soweto, a township in Johannesburg.
He shares anecdotes about his independent mother, Patricia,
who worked hard to provide for him despite the limitations placed on her by apartheid.
He also explores the complexities of identity and race,
as well as the ways in which he navigated the racial divides of South African society from a young age.

The issues addressed in “ Born a Crime”, such as racial segregation and discrimination, relate
to the history of Rwanda, particularly during the period of ethnic division and the Rwandan Genocide.
Like South Africa during apartheid, Rwanda faced deep-seated ethnic tensions that resulted in tragic consequences,
Trevor Noah’s story serves as a reminder of the importance of overcoming such divisions and working towards unity and reconciliation.
