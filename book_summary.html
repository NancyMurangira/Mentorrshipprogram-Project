Born a Crime - Chapter 1 and 2 Summary

In the first two chapters of “Born a Crime”, Trevor Noah talks about his childhood in South Africa
during apartheid. He talks about his parent’s childhood in South Africa during apartheid. He talks
about his parents’s relationship, his mother’s strictness, and his experiences navigating life as a
mixed-race child in a racially segregated society.

The issues addressed in “ Born a Crime”, such as racial segregation and discrimination, relate
to the history of Rwanda, particularly during the period of ethnic division and the Rwandan Genocide.
Like South Africa during apartheid, Rwanda faced deep-seated ethnic tensions that resulted in tragic consequences,
Trevor Noah’s story serves as a reminder of the importance of overcoming such divisions and working towards unity and reconciliation.
